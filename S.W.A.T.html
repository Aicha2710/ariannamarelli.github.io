S.W.A.T.

S.W.A.T. is an American television series produced by Aaron Rahsaan Thomas and Shawn Ryan for CBS. It is a remake of the TV series of the same name that aired from 1975 to 1976, which also gave rise to the 2003 film S.W.A.T. On March 27, 2018, the series was renewed for a second season consisting of 23 episodes.On May 9, 2019, CBS renewed the series for a third season consisting of 21 episodes. On May 23, 2020, CBS renewed the series for a fourth season consisting of 18 episodes. In April 2021, the series was renewed for a fifth season.

Plot

A S.W.A.T. sergeant from the South Los Angeles area, former Marine Daniel 'Hondo' Harrelson, is assigned to head a specialized tactical unit that represents the final stop of Los Angeles law enforcement, in the premiere of the series. Torn between loyalty to the place where he grew up and loyalty to his brothers in blue, Hondo has what it takes to be an excellent leader and to act as a bridge between his two worlds. His elite unit includes David "Deacon" Kay, an experienced S.W.A.T. agent who always puts the team first, while feeling snubbed as a leader; Jim Street, a cheeky but promising new member of the group; Christina "Chris" Alonso, skilled officer and dog trainer of the team; Dominique Luca, expert driver who takes everyone in and out of the most dangerous situations; and Victor Tan, a relatively new S.W.A.T. officer who uses his confidential community informants to help the team. Overseeing the unit is Jessica Cortez, captain of the L.A. Metro for whom the work is worth more than anything else, including her secret relationship with Hondo. Hondo's team has an intense rivalry with another Los Angeles unit led by Mumford, an aggressive leader who likes to do things his own way. With Hondo in charge, these painstaking men and women bravely put themselves at risk to protect their community and save lives.

Main characters

  • Daniel "Hondo" Harrelson Jr
    (seasons 1-ongoing), portrayed by Shemar Moore: A sergeant born in Los Angeles, he is promoted to foreman in a blatant attempt to ease tensions between the black community and the city's police. He knows many people in the neighborhood and treats them with respect. It is highly appreciated by colleagues. In season 1 he has a secret and troubled relationship with Jessica Cortez, his boss. In season 2, he has an affair with Nia, a deputy prosecutor.
  • Jessica Cortez
    (seasons 1-2), portrayed by Stephanie Sigman: Commander of the LAPD Metropolitan Division and Hondo's secret girlfriend in the first season. At the end of the second season he accepts a job at the FBI.
  • James Street
    (season 1-ongoing), played by Alex Russell: Joins the team after being transferred from the Long Beach Police Department. His mother Karen, in prison for killing her abusive husband, is released at the end of season 1. Street doesn't follow the rules much and loves speed in cars and motorcycles. At the end of season 1, he was fired by Hondo because of his mother. He will return to S.W.A.T. from episode 7 of season 2, but as a new recruit.
  • David "Deacon" Kay
    (season 1-ongoing), played by Jay Harrington: A 10-year veteran of S.W.A.T., he was to be the new boss. He is the only one on the team who is married, has three children and is a very religious Catholic. At the end of season 1, his wife reveals that she is expecting their fourth child. In the episode "Shaky Town" it is revealed that she speaks fluently French. In the episode "School", it is revealed that his daughter Lila is named after a victim of a school shooting.
  • Christina "Chris" Alonso
    (season 1-5), played by Lina Esco: Officer and dog lover, is the first woman to join S.W.A.T. He is bisexual and in season 2 will have a relationship with a couple formed by two boyfriends. In the second episode of season 2 he has an argument with Kay's wife, who does not want her children to believe that polyamory is normal and acceptable; soon he gets drunk with Street and the two kiss.
  • Dominique Luca
    (season 1-ongoing), portrayed by Kenny Johnson: A third-generation Italian-American S.W.A.T. Officer. He has a stormy relationship with the historical girlfriend he leaves during the first season. In the episode "Payback", he becomes Sergeant of Hondo. In the second season he will buy a house to renovate in the suburbs.
  • Jeffrey Mumford
    (season 1-recurring season 2, guest season 4), portrayed by Peter Onorati: Team leader of another S.W.A.T. team competing with Hondo's. He has been married three times and in the episode "Payback" of the first season he will get engaged and marry for the fourth time after only a month of acquaintance.
  • Victor Tan
    (season 1-ongoing), portrayed by David Lim: S.W.A.T. officer of Asian descent. He has a stable relationship with Bonny, at the end of season 3 asks to marry him. In season 4 he is targeted by the community to which he attributes responsibility for covid-19.
