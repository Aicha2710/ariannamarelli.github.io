The Good Place

The Good Place is an American fantasy television series created by Michael Schur. The series aired from September 19, 2016 to January 30, 2020 on NBC. The series centers on Eleanor Shellstrop (Kristen Bell), a woman who wakes up in the afterlife and meets Michael (Ted Danson) in "The Good Place", a highly selective and Heaven-like utopia that was designed by Michael, as a reward for leading a righteous life. She realizes that she was sent there by mistake and that she has to hide her morally flawed behavior and try to become an ethically better person. William Jackson Harper, Jameela Jamil and Manny Jacinto co-star like other residents of "The Good Place," along with D'Arcy Carden who plays Janet, an artificial being who helps the inhabitants. The Good Place has received positive reviews since the first episode. She was praised for her acting, screenplay, originality, setting, and tone, as well as her final twist at the end of the first season. Starting in 2017, NBC renewed the series until a fourth and final season.

Plot

After dying in a supermarket, run over by a pile of trolleys while collecting a bottle of margarita, young Eleanor finds herself catapulted into the afterlife. However, when her mentor Michael reveals to her that she is on the "good side" thanks to her good deeds and for helping many innocents get out of death row, Eleanor realizes that there was a mistake and that she was mistaken for someone else; In fact, Eleanor is not "good" at all, but a deeply selfish girl who has never done a good deed in her entire life. Stuck in a world where it's impossible to say bad words and where everyone is kind, Eleanor must hide her "bad" behavior and learn to behave like a person worthy of the "good side."
